## Project Overview
This project explores household income, expenditure, savings behavior, and financial stress across urban and rural African communities using Power BI.

The analysis was conducted as part of the Dataverse Africa Healthcare Data Analytics Internship icebreaker project. The goal was to uncover financial patterns, compare urban and rural households, and examine how digital financial access may influence savings behavior.

---

## Project Objectives
- Compare average income and expenditure between urban and rural households
- Analyze household spending patterns
- Examine the relationship between mobile money access and savings behavior
- Explore financial stress levels across households
- Build an interactive Power BI dashboard for storytelling and insight communication

## Key Insights

### 1. Urban Cost Pressure
Urban households earned more on average but also recorded significantly higher expenditures, reducing disposable income margins.

### 2. Food-Dominated Spending
Food accounted for the largest share of household expenditure (~40%), suggesting limited discretionary income and potential vulnerability to food price inflation.

### 3. Mobile Money & Savings Behavior
Households with mobile money access showed substantially higher average savings rates (13% vs 6%), indicating a strong association between digital financial access and savings behavior.

### 4. Financial Stress Patterns
Average financial stress levels were generally low across households, although some households exhibited notably higher stress indicators.
